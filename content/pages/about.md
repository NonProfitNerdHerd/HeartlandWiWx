We're Ike and Jess, two weather enthusiasts from western Wisconsin who decided that instead of sitting on the porch watching storms roll by, we'd start following them.

Heartland Chasers isn't a full-time media company. We don't have sponsors, a production crew, or a fleet of chase vehicles. Most days we're just a married couple loading snacks into a Silverado, checking radar, and seeing where the atmosphere decides to put on a show.

What started as a hobby quickly turned into an obsession. One forecast discussion leads to another, one chase turns into ten, and before long you're burning a tank of gas because Max Velocity said, "This could be an interesting day," and your brain somehow translated that into a 500-mile road trip.

## Why We Chase

Our goal isn't to be the loudest voices in storm chasing, and we're certainly not trying to chase every storm that appears on a weather map.

The reality is that Heartland Chasers is a part-time adventure. We both have careers, family, responsibilities, and all the normal things that come with real life. That means we aren't dropping everything to chase every setup from Texas to Montana. Sometimes we'll spend weeks watching storms from home. Sometimes we'll drive six hours because the forecast looks too good to ignore.

We're not professional meteorologists. We're not full-time storm chasers. We're just two people who love weather, road trips, learning new things, and experiencing nature at its most powerful.

Some days we'll make the right call.

Some days we'll miss the storm entirely.

Sometimes we'll drive hundreds of miles and come home with nothing more than a few photos, a tank full of memories, and another lesson learned.

That's storm chasing.

When we do hit the road, it's because we genuinely believe there's a chance to witness something incredible. Whether that's a beautiful supercell at sunset, a dramatic lightning display, giant hail, or hopefully someday a tornado.

In fact, if we're being honest, the entire reason Heartland Chasers exists can be summed up in one goal:

**Cross a tornado off Jess's bucket list.**

Everything else is just part of the journey.

## Meet the Team

### Ike

Ike is a certified storm spotter, technology enthusiast, weather nerd, and lifelong problem solver. By day, he works as a Knowledge Management Officer supporting the U.S. Army Reserve. By night, he's usually building dashboards, weather tools, maps, GPS systems, streaming overlays, or some other project that probably started with the phrase, "I wonder if this is possible."

He loves forecasting, radar analysis, emergency preparedness, and learning from experienced meteorologists and storm chasers. He's currently working toward expanding his amateur radio knowledge and becoming more involved with Skywarn and the broader weather community.

### Jess

Jess is a retired Navy veteran, adventurer, and the inspiration behind Heartland Chasers.

For years, seeing a tornado has been at the top of her bucket list. What started as a simple goal eventually became a shared adventure that now takes us all over the Midwest.

Jess brings common sense, patience, and perspective to every chase. While Ike is often buried in radar data and forecast discussions, she's the one making sure everyone remembers to eat, stay safe, and occasionally enjoy the journey instead of staring at weather models all day.

Together we've spent years building a life filled with travel, adventure, and stories. Storm chasing has simply become the latest chapter.

## More Than Storms

As much as we love weather, Heartland Chasers isn't really about storms.

It's about people.

We have tremendous respect for the storm chasing community and especially for the chasers, spotters, meteorologists, emergency managers, and weather enthusiasts we've come to know and consider friends. We aren't trying to compete with them or take attention away from the incredible work they do.

If anything, we hope to support it.

Storm chasing works best when people share information, help one another, and remember that everyone out there is working toward the same goal: understanding the weather and helping keep people safe.

That's the kind of community we want to be part of.

When we travel, we carry more than cameras and weather apps. Our truck is stocked with first aid supplies, emergency equipment, recovery gear, tire repair tools, spare parts, and enough miscellaneous hardware to solve at least a few roadside problems.

If another chaser gets stuck in a ditch, needs a wrench, needs a jump start, needs medical supplies, or simply needs another set of hands, we'd rather be remembered for helping than for getting the perfect video clip.

Some of the best moments in chasing don't happen under a tornado.

They happen at gas stations while comparing forecasts.

They happen over dinner after a long chase day.

They happen while swapping stories with people you've never met before who somehow feel like old friends because you all understand why you're out there.

We want Heartland Chasers to feel like a welcoming front porch for weather enthusiasts traveling through the Upper Midwest. If you're in our area, we'll happily talk forecasts, share local knowledge, recommend a place to eat, lend a hand, or just spend some time talking weather.

The storms may be what bring us together.

**The people are what keep us coming back.**

## Follow Along

Whether we're streaming live from the road, sharing photos from a chase, posting lessons learned, or laughing about another forecast that completely fell apart, we're glad you're here.

Grab a seat, watch the radar, and come chase with us.

The next great adventure is probably only one forecast discussion away.
